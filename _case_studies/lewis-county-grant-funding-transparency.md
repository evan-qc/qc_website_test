Lewis County's Planning and Community Development team pulls in state and federal grant money that funds projects across the county - more than $5 million every year from a single team. On paper the department reads like an operating expense. In reality, every dollar it spends multiplies into outside funding for the community. Quantify built the system that finally made that impact visible.

## The challenge

The department's value was hiding in plain sight. Its grant work was buried in more than 30 disconnected tracking tables spread across multiple years and staff members, with no clear way to show legislators or the public the return on the office's effort.

The team already had strong tables and knew exactly what they wanted to track. But the tracking wasn't unified across years or team members, it wasn't visible to the public, and the tables weren't relational - which made storytelling nearly impossible. The county wasn't lacking data or results; it was lacking a way to tell the story.

## What we built

We worked closely with the planning team to understand the subject matter and the working tables they already used, then designed a data model, built prototypes, and reviewed them with the subject-matter experts at every step.

We consolidated more than 30 tables down to just 7, with well-designed relationships between them, in a unified relational database built in SharePoint-hosted Excel. That workbook became the team's single place for data entry and day-to-day grant tracking.

![Grant application tracking table showing application names, applied and award dates, application status, grant program, and funded community projects, with awarded and denied applications color-coded]({{ "/assets/case-studies/lewis-county-grant-funding-01.png" | relative_url }})

We then linked that workbook to Lewis County's Azure data warehouse and built a Power BI dashboard for county leadership, along with a public-facing view that shows residents how projects get funded by winning state and federal dollars rather than local taxes.

- **A public Power BI dashboard** mapping funding across 13 municipalities and 5 county departments
- **A SharePoint Excel tracking system** with effort, success-probability, and ROI tracking
- **Grant data in the county's Azure data warehouse**, ready for further analytics
- **In-person training and Standard Operating Procedure documentation** so the team owns the system

*Technical stack: SharePoint-hosted Excel, Azure data warehouse, and Power BI.*

## The outcome

Three years of applications, once scattered across 30-plus tables, are now unified with clear award status - and the story the planning department always wanted to tell is finally visible to everyone.

**A value multiplier, made obvious.** The public dashboard shows total funding secured, mapped by location and ranked by project, making it clear that the office returns far more than it costs.

**One source of truth, two audiences.** County leadership gets the detail it needs to plan, while residents get a plain-language view of how projects get funded.

**Data the team can act on.** The consolidated system now supports personnel development, project planning, and budgeting - not just reporting.
